CoastFIRE at Age 50: The Complete Guide

By Minh · Updated 2026-04-25 · 6 min read

You're 50. Retirement is 15 years away. The CoastFIRE question is simple: how much do I need invested today so I can stop saving and still retire on time?

This guide answers that for several income levels, walks through what to prioritize at age 50 specifically, and shows how the compound math actually works at this stage of your life.

Why Age 50 Matters

At 50, you have 15 years of compounding available before traditional retirement age. Every dollar you invest today will grow to roughly 2.08× in real (after-inflation) value by 65.

The compounding leverage: $10,000 invested at age 50 becomes $20,789 by age 65 (today's dollars), assuming 5% real return. The same $10,000 invested 10 years later becomes only $12,763 — a 63% penalty for waiting a decade.

At 50, the runway is shorter but the levers are still real. Catch-up contributions (50+) add $7,500/yr to your 401(k) limit. Most importantly: every year you delay retirement past 65 reduces your portfolio target by ~6%, both because you save longer and your money compounds another year.

What to Prioritize at Age 50

  1. Take the catch-up contributions. At 50+, the 401(k) limit jumps by $7,500 and the IRA by $1,000.
  2. Project your tax brackets in retirement. At 50, you can model whether to lean more Traditional (deduct now, taxed later) or Roth (taxed now, free later) based on expected retirement income.
  3. Consider Roth conversions if you have significant Traditional balances and expect higher tax brackets later (RMDs at 73 surprise people).
  4. Build cash reserves equivalent to 1–2 years of spending. Sequence-of-returns risk is real now.
  5. Decide your retirement age with intent. Working to 67 or 70 reduces your portfolio target dramatically and increases Social Security.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's a typical CoastFIRE number at age 50?
It depends entirely on your retirement spending. For a $75,000-income lifestyle (~$45,000/yr in retirement), CoastFIRE at 50 is approximately $541,144. For a $150,000 income (~$90,000/yr in retirement), it's around $1,082,288. The full table above shows other income levels.
Is 50 too late to start saving?
Late, yes — impossible, no. With 15 years and the 50+ catch-up contributions, you can still reach CoastFIRE before 65, especially if you're willing to consider a part-time "BaristaFIRE" approach to bridge the final years.
How does 50 compare to starting at 25?
Someone who hit CoastFIRE at 25 with a $300k portfolio would have roughly $1016k by age 50 without adding a dollar — that's the cost of waiting. The good news: starting at 50 just means a higher savings rate and tighter spending control, not failure.
What return assumption is realistic?
This page uses 5% real return (7% nominal minus 3% inflation). That matches the long-run S&P 500 average and is what most fee-only financial planners use. Some FIRE writers prefer 4% real (more conservative); a few use 7% real (more aggressive). Real-world Monte Carlo testing is in the calculator.
